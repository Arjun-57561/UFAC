PM-KISAN Scheme (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi)

The PM-KISAN scheme is a central government income-support programme for landholding farmer families in India. It provides direct financial assistance to help farmers meet agricultural expenses such as seeds, fertilizers, irrigation, labour, and other basic needs.

It was announced in December 2018 and formally launched on 24 February 2019.

1. Main Objective

The main aim of PM-KISAN is to provide a fixed amount of financial support directly to farmers so that they do not have to depend entirely on loans or informal borrowing for crop-related expenses.

Key objectives include:

Supporting small and marginal farmers
Providing assured supplementary income
Reducing dependence on moneylenders
Helping farmers buy seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, etc.
Improving agricultural productivity and rural stability
2. Amount Given Under the Scheme

Under PM-KISAN, every eligible farmer family receives:

₹6,000 per year
Paid in 3 equal instalments
₹2,000 per instalment
Money is transferred directly into the beneficiary’s bank account through DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer)

The three instalment periods are:

Instalment	Period
1st	April – July
2nd	August – November
3rd	December – March

3. Latest Status in 2026

The 22nd instalment of PM-KISAN was released in March 2026. More than ₹18,640 crore was transferred to over 9.32 crore farmer families across India.

A short timeline of recent instalments:

4. Who Is Eligible?

A farmer family is eligible if:

They are Indian citizens
They own cultivable agricultural land
The land is recorded in government land records
The family consists of husband, wife, and minor children
Aadhaar is linked to the application and bank account
e-KYC is completed

Initially the scheme was only for small and marginal farmers, but later it was expanded to all landholding farmer families irrespective of land size.

5. Who Is NOT Eligible?

The following categories are excluded from PM-KISAN:

Institutional landholders
Income-tax payers
Serving or retired government employees (except Class IV/Multi-Tasking staff)
Professionals such as:
Doctors
Engineers
Lawyers
Chartered Accountants
Architects
Pensioners receiving more than ₹10,000 per month
Current or former MPs, MLAs, Ministers, Mayors, etc.
Farmers who do not own land
Tenant farmers and sharecroppers without legal ownership records

6. Documents Required

To apply for PM-KISAN, the farmer generally needs:

Aadhaar Card
Bank account details
IFSC code
Land ownership documents
Mobile number
Residence proof
7. How to Apply

Farmers can apply in three ways:

Through the official PM-KISAN portal
Through Common Service Centres (CSC)
Through local agriculture department offices or village-level officers

The official portal is:

PM-KISAN Official Portal

8. Step-by-Step Online Registration Process
Open the PM-KISAN website
Go to “New Farmer Registration”
Enter:
Aadhaar number
Mobile number
State
Verify using OTP
Fill in personal details and land details
Upload required documents
Submit the application
Wait for approval by state authorities
9. e-KYC Requirement

e-KYC is mandatory. Farmers who do not complete e-KYC may stop receiving instalments.

Methods to complete e-KYC:

OTP-based e-KYC on the PM-KISAN website
Biometric e-KYC at CSC centres
Face-authentication through the PM-KISAN mobile app

10. How to Check Beneficiary Status

A farmer can check whether payment has been credited by:

Visiting the PM-KISAN portal
Selecting “Beneficiary Status”
Entering:
Aadhaar number, or
Mobile number, or
Registration number
Viewing payment history and instalment status

The status page shows:

Whether the application is approved
Whether instalment is released
Whether e-KYC is complete
Whether Aadhaar-bank linking is successful
11. Common Reasons for Rejection or Delay

A farmer may not receive the instalment because of:

e-KYC not completed
Aadhaar not linked with bank account
Wrong bank account number or IFSC
Land record mismatch
Duplicate application
Ineligible category
Spelling mismatch between Aadhaar and bank details

Recent beneficiary numbers decreased slightly because the government carried out stricter verification and removed ineligible or duplicate entries.

12. Important Features of PM-KISAN
Fully funded by Government of India
100% Direct Benefit Transfer
No middlemen
Transparent digital system
Benefits are directly credited to bank accounts
Covers crores of farmers across India
Linked with Aadhaar and bank verification
13. Advantages of the Scheme
Economic Benefits
Helps farmers buy inputs on time
Reduces financial pressure
Supports small and marginal farmers
Social Benefits
Improves rural living conditions
Increases confidence among farmers
Promotes financial inclusion
Agricultural Benefits
Better crop preparation
Timely purchase of seeds and fertilizer
Less dependency on private lenders
14. Major Criticisms and Challenges

Although PM-KISAN is widely used, some issues still exist:

Tenant farmers and landless farmers are excluded
₹6,000 per year may be too low for large farming expenses
Land record errors cause delays
Some states take time to verify applications
Many farmers are unaware of e-KYC requirements
15. PM-KISAN and Other Related Schemes

PM-KISAN is often linked with other farmer schemes such as:

Kisan Credit Card
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana
PM-KUSUM
Soil Health Card Scheme

Many farmers receiving PM-KISAN also use these schemes for loans, insurance, soil testing, and solar irrigation.
